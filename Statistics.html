Statistics

11 teens die every day as a result of texting while driving

The National Safety Council reports that cell phone use while driving leads to 1.6 million crashes each year

Nearly 330,000 injuries occur each year from accidents caused by texting while driving

Texting while driving is 6x more likely to cause an accident than driving drunk

21% of teen drivers involved in fatal accidents were distracted by their cell phones

The Virginia Tech Transportation Institute found that text messaging creates a crash risk 23 times worse than driving while not distracted

1 out of every 4 car accidents in the United States is caused by texting and driving

Teens who text while driving spend approximately 10% of their driving time outside of their lane

In 2014, 3,179 people were killed, and 431,000 were injured in motor vehicle crashes involving distracted drivers
